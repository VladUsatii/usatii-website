## Problem

The client had fragmented posting workflows, inconsistent editing quality, and too many manual handoffs.

Their output quality could be described as

$$
Q = f(c, e, d, s)
$$

where $c$ is content planning quality, $e$ is editing consistency, $d$ is distribution reliability, and $s$ is system tightness.

## Intervention

We replaced the scattered workflow with a single publishing system:

- one planning lane
- one editing lane
- one approval rhythm
- one publishing calendar

## Outcome

The retained-output model improved materially. A simplified representation looked like

$$
R_t = \alpha R_{t-1} + \beta S_t - \gamma F_t
$$

where:

- $R_t$ is retained audience quality at time $t$
- $S_t$ is system consistency
- $F_t$ is operational friction

## Notes

Inline math also works, such as $O(n \log n)$ versus $O(n^2)$ comparisons.
